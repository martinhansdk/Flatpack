## Short Description (160 characters max)

Export flat faces to DXF or SVG for laser cutting. No sketches needed. Color-coded cuts, batch export, persistent settings. Fast and simple.

## Long Description

### Streamline Your Laser Cutting Workflow

Flatpack simplifies exporting flat faces from Fusion 360 to DXF and SVG files for laser cutting, CNC routing, and other fabrication processes. No more creating sketches for every face you want to export!

### Key Features

** Simplified Workflow**
- Export faces directly without creating sketches
- Select multiple faces and export to a single file
- Settings are saved with your document for easy re-export

** Smart Color Coding**
- Outer edges and inner holes use different colors
- Cut holes first to prevent material shifting
- Works perfectly with most laser cutter software

** Automatic Layout**
- Parts are automatically oriented for efficient cutting
- Organized in a horizontal row with spacing
- No manual arrangement needed

** Perfect for Iterations**
- Settings persist in your document
- Re-export after design changes with one click
- Maintains face selections automatically

** Flexible Export Options**
- Export to DXF or SVG format
- Adjustable curve conversion tolerance
- Compatible with most CAM software

### Who Is This For?

- **Makers & Hobbyists** creating laser-cut projects
- **Product Designers** prototyping with sheet materials
- **Engineers** exporting parts for fabrication
- **Anyone** tired of Fusion 360's built-in DXF export workflow

### How It Works

1. Select the flat faces you want to export
2. Choose your output file and format (.dxf or .svg)
3. Click OK - done!

Curves are automatically converted to line segments with adjustable precision. Inner holes and outer edges are color-coded for easy identification in your cutting software.

### Technical Details

- **Supported Platforms:** Windows 10/11, macOS 10.15+
- **Fusion 360 Version:** Any recent version
- **Output Formats:** DXF (R12 compatible), SVG 1.1
- **Units:** DXF in millimeters, SVG in centimeters
- **File Size:** Lightweight, minimal performance impact

### Privacy & Security

Flatpack only stores export settings in your Fusion 360 document. No data is transmitted to external servers. No telemetry. No tracking. See our full privacy policy for details.

## Installation Instructions (for App Store)

### Automatic Installation (via App Store)
1. Click "Install" in the Autodesk App Store
2. Open Fusion 360
3. Find the command under Utilities > Make > Export faces to DXF or SVG
4. Start exporting!

### Manual Installation
1. Download the add-in bundle
2. In Fusion 360, go to Utilities > Add-Ins
3. Click the + button and browse to the Flatpack.bundle folder
4. Enable the add-in and check "Run on Startup"

## FAQ for Listing

**Q: Is this free?**
A: Yes, Flatpack is completely free and open source.

**Q: What file formats are supported?**
A: DXF and SVG. Both are widely compatible with laser cutters and CAM software.

**Q: Does it work with curved surfaces?**
A: No, only flat (planar) faces can be exported. Curves on flat faces are converted to line segments.

**Q: Can I control the part layout?**
A: Parts are automatically arranged in a horizontal row. For custom nesting, use dedicated nesting software after export.

**Q: Does it send my data anywhere?**
A: No. Flatpack only stores settings in your local Fusion 360 document. Nothing is transmitted externally.

**Q: Is it compatible with Fusion 360 for Web?**
A: No, add-ins only work in the desktop version of Fusion 360.

**Q: Can I use this commercially?**
A: Yes, the MIT license permits commercial use.
